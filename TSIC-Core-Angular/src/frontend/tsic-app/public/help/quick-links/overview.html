Quick Links

These are the master switches for what your event's public landing page offers — which registration buttons and calls-to-action are live. Flip a switch on and that path appears to visitors; flip it off and it's hidden. It's the fastest place to open or close registration and features for the event.

Each switch saves the moment you flip it.

There's no separate save — a toggle is live immediately, so a switch you turn off vanishes from the public page right away.

What the switches control

Each toggle exposes or hides one thing on the landing page — player registration, team registration, coach sign-up, the store, and so on. Turning a switch on is how families and clubs get a button to start; leaving it off keeps that path closed until you're ready.

When a switch is greyed out

  • Disabled with a reason — the switch doesn't apply to this job type, or its fees aren't set up yet. It stays visible (so you can see its state) but you can't flip it until the underlying setup is done.
  • "SuperUser only" — a few switches (like insurance or store) are reserved for SuperUsers; you'll see them but can't change them.
  • A caution when ON — some switches warn about a consequence (for example, opening coach registration when no teams exist yet). It's a heads-up, not a block — you can leave it on if that's intentional.

Same flags, one convenient place

These switches also live in their proper Configure Job tabs — this screen just gathers the public-facing ones in one spot for quick access. Changing one here is the same as changing it there.

Common mistakes

  • Registration "isn't working" — but the switch is off. If families can't register, check that its toggle is on here first. This is the most common cause.
  • Leaving a path open too long. A switch left on keeps accepting sign-ups; turn it off when a registration window closes.
  • Expecting a greyed switch to flip. It's disabled for a reason shown right on it — fix that (job type, fees, or role) first.
