Rescheduler

The grid is empty until you choose filters.

Pick an age group, division, day, or field and the schedule loads for that slice — that's the starting step, not a bug.

Why is the schedule grid blank when I open the page?

By design — the Rescheduler waits for you to filter to the games you want before loading anything. Select a League (or Club) branch, and optionally a game day or field, and the grid fills in a moment later.

How do I move a game to a new slot?

Select the game and drop it on an open slot to move it, or drop it on another game to swap the two. If the slot you need doesn't exist, use Add Timeslot to create a row at a specific date and time first.

A whole day got pushed back by weather. Do I move every game?

No — use the weather adjustment. Set a New First Game Time and the day's games shift together to the later start, so one action re-times the entire day instead of dozens of manual moves.

It warned me about a time clash. What does that mean?

The slot you're moving into would put a team in two places at once — one of its games already sits in that timeslot. The warning is your cue to pick a different slot before you commit the move.

How do the teams find out a game moved?

Send them a note from here: fill in the Subject and Message Body to dispatch a schedule-change notification to the affected teams. Moving the game and notifying are separate actions — do both so nobody shows up at the old time.

Should I build a new schedule here?

No. The Rescheduler is for moving games that already exist. To generate games, rebuild a division, or delete a build, use the Schedule Hub.

Tip: filter down to the single day or field you're changing before you start moving games. A tight grid makes the open slots obvious and keeps you from dropping a game into the wrong division.
