Rescheduler

Once a schedule is built, this is where you move games around — a rain delay, a field that went unplayable, two games that need to swap slots. You filter to the games you care about, then drag them to new times or fields, and can notify the affected teams in the same place.

Pick filters first — the grid loads nothing until you do.

Choose an age group, division, team, day, or field and the schedule loads for just that slice. It's a deliberate "tell me what you're working on" step.

Loading the games you want

  • Filter by League (age group → division → team) or by Club — whichever way you're thinking about the games.
  • Add Game Days and Fields filters to focus a single day or complex.
  • The grid loads automatically a moment after you change filters.

Moving and swapping games

  • Select a game, then drop it on an open slot to move it.
  • Drop it on another game to swap the two.
  • Need a slot that doesn't exist yet? Add Timeslot to create a row at a specific date and time, then move a game into it.
  • The grid warns you about a time clash — a team already playing in that slot — before you create a conflict.

Weather adjustment

When a day starts late, you don't have to move every game by hand. Set a New First Game Time and the day's games shift together to the new start — a one-move fix for a rain delay across a whole day.

Tell the teams

A change nobody hears about causes no-shows. Use the Subject and Message Body to send a schedule-change notification to the affected teams right after you make the move.

Common mistakes

  • Waiting for the grid to appear on its own. It won't — select filters first.
  • Moving into a clash. Heed the time-clash warning; putting two of a team's games in one slot creates a conflict you'll have to undo.
  • Forgetting to notify. Moving the game and telling the teams are two steps — do the second one.
  • Using this to build a schedule. The Rescheduler moves existing games; to generate or rebuild games, use the Schedule Hub.
