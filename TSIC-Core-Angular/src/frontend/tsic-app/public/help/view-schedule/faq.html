Missing games? A filter is almost always the reason.

Check the filter chips at the top and clear the ones you don't want — that's the fix for "where did my games go?"

How do I enter or fix a score?

On the Games tab, enter the score right on the game as an admin. Standings and brackets recalculate from it. To change more than the score, open the game and edit its details.

What's the fastest way to find games that still need scores?

Turn on the Unscored filter — it hides everything that already has a result, leaving just the games you still need to enter. Work straight down that list at the end of a round.

What's the difference between filtering By Club and By League?

Two paths to the same games. By Club drills Club → Age Group → Division → Team — handy when a club asks about their schedule. By League drills the event's own League → Age Group → Division → Team structure. Use whichever matches how you're thinking about the games.

My standings look wrong. What should I check?

Standings are computed entirely from entered scores, so a missing or mistyped score is the usual culprit. Use the Unscored filter to spot games without a result, and re-check any score that looks off on the Games tab.

How do I move a game to a different time or field?

Not here — this screen records results and edits game details. To relocate a game to another timeslot or field, use the Rescheduler, which is built for moving games (including weather shifts).

Is this the same schedule the public sees?

Yes — it's the same games and standings, published on the public schedule page. The difference is that as an admin you also get score entry and game editing; the public view is read-only.

Tip: keep scores current during the event. Because standings and brackets are computed live from them, entering results as games finish means seeding and playoff pictures are always right — no end-of-day catch-up scramble.
