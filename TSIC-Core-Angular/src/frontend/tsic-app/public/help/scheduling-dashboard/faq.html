Scheduling Dashboard

Work the pipeline cards top to bottom.

Each links to the screen that turns it green. When they're all green, you're ready to build.

What do the pipeline cards actually tell me?

Each card is one prerequisite for building a schedule — LADT setup, pool assignment, fields, pairings, timeslots — with a live count and a color for how complete it is. Click a card to jump to the screen where you finish that step.

Where's the actual "build the schedule" button?

Under Schedule Builder, which opens the Schedule Hub. This dashboard gets you ready; the Hub is where you generate the games and place championships.

What are the Post-Scheduling Tools for?

They're the after-the-build screens: View Schedule to review and enter scores, Rescheduler to move games, and the Schedule QA Report to validate. Use them once games exist.

A card is yellow or empty. Is that blocking me?

It's telling you that step isn't finished. A build needs the pipeline complete, so an empty Fields or Pairings card is exactly what would stop the builder or produce a thin schedule. Click the card and finish it before building.

Tip: for a returning event, the cards are the fastest way to confirm last year's setup carried forward — a quick glance shows whether fields, pairings, and timeslots are already green before you build.
