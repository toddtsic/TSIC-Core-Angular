Push Notification

Every push interrupts every device — there's no way to un-send it.

Read it twice before you click Send. It goes to all mobile users at once, immediately.

Who receives a push notification?

Everyone using this event's mobile app who has notifications turned on. The device count at the top of the page is your live reach — that's exactly how many phones the next push will hit.

Why is the device count lower than my registrant count?

Push only reaches people who installed the mobile app and allowed notifications — that's always a subset of everyone registered. For full coverage, pair an urgent push with an email or bulletin so people without the app still get the word.

How long should the message be?

One short line. Phones truncate long push text, so lead with the essential fact — "Fields closed, games delayed to 2pm" beats a full paragraph nobody sees the end of.

Can I edit or recall a push after sending?

No. Once sent, it's on people's phones. If you made a mistake, send a brief follow-up correction rather than trying to undo the first one.

Did my last push go out?

Check the Sent Push Notifications list below the form — it shows every push newest-first with the sender, time, device count, and text. If it's there, it sent.

Tip: save push for genuinely urgent, everyone-affected news — weather, delays, field moves. Overuse trains people to swipe your alerts away, and then the one that matters gets ignored too.
