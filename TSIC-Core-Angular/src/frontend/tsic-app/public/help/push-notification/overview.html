Push Notification

Send a short alert straight to the phones of everyone running your event's mobile app. Unlike email, a push pops up on the device immediately — use it for time-sensitive, everyone-needs-to-know moments like a weather delay, a field change, or "games resume in 15 minutes."

A push goes to every registered device at once.

There's no audience picker — it reaches all mobile users for this event. Send it only for things that truly concern everyone.

Sending one

  1. Check the device count at the top — that's how many phones will receive the message right now.
  2. Type a short message in the Push Text box. Keep it to a sentence — push alerts truncate, and long ones lose their punch.
  3. Click Send Push Notification. You'll get a confirmation, and the box clears.

History

Every push you've sent is listed below the form — newest first — with who sent it, when, the device count at the time, and the text. Use it to confirm a message went out and to avoid sending duplicates.

Common mistakes

  • Writing a paragraph. Push text should be one short line; anything longer gets cut off on the device.
  • Using it for routine info. A push interrupts everyone's phone — for non-urgent updates use a bulletin or email instead.
  • Expecting it to reach people without the app. Only devices that installed the event's mobile app and opted into notifications receive a push — the device count is your real reach.
