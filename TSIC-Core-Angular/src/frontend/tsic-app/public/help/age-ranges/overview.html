Age Ranges

Age ranges define who is eligible for each age group by birthdate. Each range is a named band — like U12, U14, or Open — with a date-of-birth window. When a family registers, their player's birthdate is checked against these ranges to confirm they fit the age group they're signing up for.

A range is a name plus a birthdate window.

"U12" isn't just a label here — it's a DOB From and DOB To that decide exactly who qualifies.

What each range holds

  • Range Name — the label players and admins see (e.g. U12, U14, Open).
  • DOB From / DOB To — the earliest and latest birthdate that qualifies for the range.

Add, edit, or remove ranges as your event's divisions require.

Getting the dates right

The birthdate window is the whole point — set it to your governing body's cutoff rules for the season. A range that's too wide lets over-age players in; too narrow turns away players who should qualify. Double-check the DOB From and DOB To against the official age cutoffs before you rely on them.

When you'll see this

Age ranges apply when an event uses age-based team eligibility. If your event doesn't gate by age, you won't need to configure them.

Common mistakes

  • Off-by-a-year windows. Age cutoffs are unforgiving — a DOB boundary one day or one year off admits or rejects the wrong players.
  • Overlapping ranges. If two ranges cover the same birthdate, eligibility gets ambiguous. Keep the windows clean and non-overlapping.
  • Naming without dating. The name is cosmetic; the DOB window does the work. A correctly-named range with the wrong dates still lets the wrong players in.
