Age Ranges

The birthdate window is what enforces eligibility — not the name.

"U12" only means what its DOB From/To say. Get those dates right against your official age cutoffs.

What is an age range, exactly?

A named eligibility band with a birthdate window — a Range Name (U12, U14, Open) plus a DOB From and DOB To. A player qualifies for the range when their birthdate falls inside that window.

A player was accepted (or rejected) for the wrong age group. Why?

Almost always the range's DOB From/To dates. Eligibility is decided by that window, so an off-by-one boundary admits over-age players or turns away ones who should qualify. Check the dates against your governing body's cutoff for the season.

Can ranges overlap?

Avoid it. If two ranges cover the same birthdate, it's ambiguous which age group a player belongs to. Keep each range's window distinct so every birthdate maps cleanly to one band.

Do I always need to set these up?

Only when your event uses age-based team eligibility. If the event doesn't gate by age, age ranges don't apply and you can leave them alone.

Is renaming a range enough to change who qualifies?

No — the name is just a label. To change who's eligible you have to change the DOB From/To window. A range renamed without adjusting its dates still admits exactly the same players.

Tip: set your age ranges from your governing body's published cutoff dates for the season, and re-check them each year — the birthdate boundaries shift as the season rolls forward.
