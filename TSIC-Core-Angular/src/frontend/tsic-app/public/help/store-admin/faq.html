Store Admin

Colors × sizes = SKUs.

An item's sellable variations are generated automatically from the colors and sizes you attach. Get those lists right first, and the SKU matrix builds itself.

What's a SKU, and where do they come from?

A SKU is one sellable variation of an item — a specific color and size, like "Hoodie / Navy / Large." When you create an item and pick its colors and sizes, the store generates a SKU for every combination automatically. Expand an item to see them.

How do I stop selling something without deleting it?

Toggle it inactive. You can deactivate a whole item, or just one SKU (say, only the small size sold out). It disappears from the storefront but keeps its history and can be switched back on.

Why won't it let me delete a color or size?

Because a SKU still uses it — deleting would orphan that variation. Remove the color/size from the items that use it first, or simply leave it in place. Unused colors and sizes do no harm.

What does "max can sell" do?

It's your inventory cap. At the item level it limits total sales; at the SKU level it limits that specific color/size. Once a SKU hits its max, buyers can't order more of it — set these to what you actually have in stock.

I added an item but it's not in the store. Why?

Check three things: the item is active, it has at least one active SKU, and the store itself is enabled for the event. Any one of those being off will hide it from shoppers.

Where do I see how sales are going?

The Analytics tab. It summarizes store performance so you can see what's moving and what isn't without digging through orders.

Tip: build your colors and sizes lists once, up front. After that, adding a new item is just naming it, pricing it, and ticking the variations it comes in — the SKUs handle themselves.
