Bulletins

A bulletin shows only when it's active AND inside its date window.

Miss either one and it stays hidden — that's the answer to almost every "why isn't my bulletin showing?"

I saved a bulletin but it's not on the home page. Why?

Check the two gates. The Active switch must be on, and today must be between the bulletin's Start Date and End Date. A bulletin that's inactive, or dated for next week, won't appear yet.

How do I take a bulletin down?

Turn its Active switch off — it stops showing immediately but stays in the list so you can reuse it later. Set an End Date if you'd rather it retire itself on a schedule. Delete only when you're sure you won't want it again.

Who sees these bulletins?

Anyone who visits your event's home page while the bulletin is active and in its date window. Treat them as public — they aren't private notes or targeted messages.

What's the Resolved Preview and the Simulate checkboxes?

The Resolved Preview renders the bulletin exactly as visitors will see it, with any tokens filled in. Because some content only appears under certain conditions, the Simulate checkboxes let you preview those variations without changing the real event settings. Check the preview before you save.

What are tokens for?

Tokens insert dynamic text into the content so a bulletin can adapt rather than being fully hand-typed. Insert one from the token bar and use the Resolved Preview to confirm it fills in the way you expect.

Can it help me write the bulletin?

Yes — describe what you want to announce and the editor can draft a title and content for you. It's a starting point; edit the wording and always preview before you make it active.

Tip: use the End Date for time-bound notices — a check-in reminder or a weather update can retire itself automatically, so your home page never carries a stale announcement.
