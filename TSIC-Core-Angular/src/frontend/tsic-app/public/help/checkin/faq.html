Check-In

Check-in is quick and reversible.

Tap to check someone in, tap Undo to reverse it — nothing here is locked, so work fast.

Should I use Teams or Players mode?

The screen picks a default from your event type — Teams for tournaments and leagues, Players for camps and tryouts. If that's not the view you want, switch modes at the top. Team mode checks in a whole team at once; player mode checks in individuals under a team.

I checked in the wrong team/player. How do I fix it?

Undo it from that row — check-in stamps a time but isn't permanent, so reversing a mistaken tap takes one click. The checked-in count updates accordingly.

The camp roster is huge. How do I find one player fast?

Use the search box in player mode — it filters by name, club, or school as you type, so you don't scroll a list of hundreds. Pick the player's team first, then search within it.

Can I tell if a registrant still owes money at check-in?

Yes — open their detail panel (the same fly-in from Registration Search) to see their information and payment status right at the table. It's the moment to catch an outstanding balance before someone heads out to play.

How do I know how many have checked in?

The count at the top tallies checked-in rows for the current mode — a live read on how far through arrivals you are. It updates every time you check someone in or undo.

What's the roommate column I sometimes see?

It's a camp-only field, shown only when players on the loaded roster actually have a roommate preference. On tournament or non-camp rosters it stays hidden so the table stays clean.

Tip: before the event, decide who's running check-in and on what device. Any director-level admin can run this screen, and because it's reversible, more than one person can work arrivals at once without stepping on each other.
