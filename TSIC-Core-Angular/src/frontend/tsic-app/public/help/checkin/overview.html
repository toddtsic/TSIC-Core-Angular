Check-In

This is your day-of, on-site check-in screen — mark teams or players as arrived as they come through. It's built to be fast under pressure: tap a row to check someone in, and a running count shows how many are done.

Two modes: Teams or Players

The screen opens in the mode that fits your event — Teams for tournaments and leagues, Players for camps and tryouts — and you can switch modes at the top if you need the other view.

Teams

One row per team. Check the whole team in as it arrives.

Players

Pick a team, then check in individual players. Best for camps, where you check people in one by one.

Checking people in

  • Tap a row's check-in control to mark it arrived — it stamps the time and updates the checked-in count at the top.
  • Checked the wrong row? Undo it — check-in isn't permanent, so a mis-tap is a one-click fix.
  • In player mode, use the search box to jump to a name, club, or school — camp rosters can run into the hundreds.

See details before you wave someone through

Open a registrant's detail panel (the same fly-in used in Registration Search) to check their information and payment status right at the table. If someone still owes a balance, you'll see it here before they head onto the field.

Check-in is a good moment to catch an unpaid balance.

The detail panel shows what's owed — a quick glance before checking someone in keeps money issues from slipping past.

Common mistakes

  • Working in the wrong mode. If you expected a player list but see teams (or vice-versa), switch the mode at the top — it defaults to the event type but you can override it.
  • Scrolling a huge roster. Don't — type in the search box to find the player instantly.
  • Checking in the wrong row. Use Undo; nothing is locked in.
