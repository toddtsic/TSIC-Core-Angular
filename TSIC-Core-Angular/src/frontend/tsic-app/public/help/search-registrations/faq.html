Searching registrations

Filters don't apply until you press Search.

Set your criteria, then press . Closing the drawer with unapplied changes prompts you to run it.

I changed my filters but the results didn't update.

Filters take effect only when you press Search. Active filters then show as removable chips above the results, so you can always see — and clear — what's applied.

A filter changed the role for me. Is that a bug?

No. A few filters narrow the role because they only make sense for one kind of registrant — an insurance filter implies players, for example. Some filters also appear only when the event uses that feature (RegSaver insurance, or ARB recurring billing).

What do the red amount and calendar icon in the Owed column mean?

A red amount means a balance is due; a calendar icon means an automatic payment is scheduled for an upcoming date. The footer totals Paid and Owed across the whole result set.

Why are some rows struck through?

Those registrations are inactive. The strikethrough sets them apart from active rows.

How do I email just some people, not everyone?

Check the rows you want and use Email Selected to open the batch composer for those people. Email All composes to the entire result set.

Where do I refund a card payment or delete a registration?

Click a person's name to open their detail panel. From there you can update contact info and demographics, process a credit-card refund, or delete the registration.

Why don't I see the Invite button?

Two things have to be true. Your search must be scoped to a single invitable role — Player or Club Rep — since an invitation is tied to that role, and there must be another event you're eligible to invite them to. If either isn't the case, the button stays hidden.

What does "Look up CCs expiring this month" do?

For events with recurring billing, it queries the card processor live and returns everyone with a card about to expire — deliberately ignoring your current filters — so you can collect balances before an automatic payment fails.

Tip: the filter chips above the results are your safety check. If a search looks wrong, check the chips before assuming the data is off — usually a stray filter is still applied.
