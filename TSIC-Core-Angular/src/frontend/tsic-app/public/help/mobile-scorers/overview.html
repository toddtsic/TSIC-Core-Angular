Mobile Scorers

Manage the accounts that let people enter live game scores from the mobile scoring app. Each scorer here is a login you hand to a field marshal, volunteer, or official so they can post results in real time — which is what keeps standings and brackets current during the event.

A scorer account is a scoped login for the app, not full admin access.

Scorers can enter scores from their phone; they can't get into the rest of the admin system.

Managing accounts

  • Add a scorer to create a login (a username and credential) to give to whoever's entering scores.
  • Edit an account to update its details, and switch it Active on or off to grant or revoke access without deleting it.
  • Delete an account you no longer need.

How it fits the event

Scores entered through the mobile app flow straight into the schedule — the same results you'd type on the View Schedule screen. Setting up scorers ahead of time means your on-site crew can keep the scoreboard live without an admin at a laptop.

Common mistakes

  • Not deactivating after the event. Flip accounts to inactive (or delete them) once the tournament's over so old logins can't post scores later.
  • Sharing one account for everyone. Separate accounts make it clear who entered what — create one per scorer where you can.
  • Expecting scorers to see admin tools. Their access is limited to scoring; anything more is done from the admin site.
