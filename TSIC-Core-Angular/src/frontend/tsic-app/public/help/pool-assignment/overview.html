Team Pool Assignment

This is where you sort teams into divisions — the pools they play their round-robin games in. Like the Roster Swapper, it's two panels side by side: pick a division on the left and another on the right, then move teams between them. Divisions are grouped by age group in each picker.

A division is a pool.

How you split teams here decides who plays whom in pool play — and it feeds scheduling and bracket seeding downstream.

Moving teams

  • Choose the two divisions you're balancing — one in each panel.
  • Select teams in either panel and transfer them toward the other side. It works both directions.
  • Use Filter teams… and the sortable columns to find teams quickly on a crowded division.

Preview before you commit

Moves go through a Transfer Preview first, so you can see exactly what's about to happen — including any Club Rep Impact — before confirming. Two outcomes are possible:

Transfer

A straight move — the team leaves one division and joins the other.

Symmetrical swap

When the target division is full, the preview asks you to send a team back the other way, so both pools stay within size.

Balancing the pools

The team rows carry ranking columns — for lacrosse events, national rank, rating, and record — so you can spread strong teams across pools instead of stacking them in one. Sort by rank to see the balance at a glance before you move anyone.

Common mistakes

  • Unbalanced pools. Dropping all the top teams into one division makes for a lopsided event — use the ranking columns to spread them.
  • Ignoring the preview. The preview is where a simple move turns out to need a swap, or flags a club-rep impact. Read it before confirming.
  • Reorganizing pools after scheduling. Divisions drive the games and the seeds — moving teams after games are built means rebuilding. Get pools right first.
