Team Pool Assignment

Divisions are the pools your teams play in.

Sort teams into divisions here, and scheduling builds round-robin games within each one.

How is this different from the Roster Swapper?

Same two-panel layout, different thing being moved. The Roster Swapper moves players between teams; this screen moves whole teams between divisions (pools). Here you're shaping the competition structure, not a team's roster.

What is a "symmetrical swap" and why is it asking for one?

The division you're moving a team into is already full. To keep both pools within size, the preview asks you to send a team back the other way — a one-for-one swap instead of a plain transfer. Pick the team to return, and confirm.

What does the Transfer Preview show me?

Exactly what the move will do before it happens: which teams change divisions, whether a swap is required, and any Club Rep Impact. Nothing changes until you confirm from the preview, so it's a safe place to check your work.

How do I keep the pools balanced?

Sort by the Rank / rating columns and spread the stronger teams across divisions rather than stacking them together. On lacrosse events those columns come from national ranking data, so you can balance by real strength before you move anyone.

I don't see the division I want in the picker.

Divisions are grouped by age group in the dropdown — scroll to the right age group to find it. If a division doesn't exist yet, create it in the L-A-D-T Editor first, then come back to populate it.

Can I reassign pools after the schedule is built?

You can move teams, but it's disruptive — divisions drive the round-robin games and the bracket seeds, so changing them after scheduling means rebuilding the affected games. Settle your pools before you generate the schedule.

Tip: get pools right before you build the schedule. Pool assignment is the foundation the round-robin games and playoff seeds are built on — settling it first saves a rebuild later.
