QA Results

This is the schedule's report card. After you build, it runs a comprehensive set of checks and tells you whether the schedule is clean, has warnings worth a look, or has hard errors you must fix before publishing. Run it before you hand a schedule to anyone.

Three levels of finding

Errors

Things that break the event — a field or team booked twice at once, a seeding rank mismatch, a broken bracket. Fix these.

Warnings

Things to review — a team with no games, back-to-back games, repeated matchups, an inactive team still in a game.

Summaries

Balance checks — games per team, game spread, field utilization. Informational, for fairness.

The critical checks (must-fix)

  • Field double bookings — two games on the same field at the same time.
  • Team double bookings — a team scheduled to play two games at once.
  • Rank mismatches — a bracket seed pointing at a rank the pool can't produce.
  • Bracket structural errors — a playoff bracket that doesn't hang together.

These are the ones that stop play or produce a wrong bracket — clear them before publishing.

Warnings worth a look

  • Unscheduled teams — active teams with zero games. Almost always a real problem.
  • Back-to-back games and repeated matchups — usually fine, sometimes not; judge by your event's rules.
  • Inactive teams in games — a dropped team still on the grid.

Reading and acting on it

The top badge shows the overall verdict and a total issue count; expand any section to see the specific games. QA only reports — you fix issues where they live: rebuild or delete in the Schedule Hub, move a game in the Rescheduler, or correct seeding in Bracket Seeds. Then re-run QA to confirm it's clean.

Common mistakes

  • Publishing on warnings without reading them. A warning like "team has zero games" is often a genuine miss.
  • Treating summaries as errors. Uneven game counts may be expected; they're there for fairness, not to block you.
  • Not re-running after a fix. QA reflects the last build — re-run it once you've made changes.
