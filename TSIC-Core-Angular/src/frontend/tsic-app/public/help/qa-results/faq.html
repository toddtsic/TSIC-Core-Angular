QA Results

Errors must be fixed; warnings must be read.

A red error stops play or breaks a bracket. A yellow warning may be fine — but "team has zero games" often isn't. Don't publish without checking both.

What's the difference between an error and a warning here?

Errors are things that break the event — a field or team booked twice at once, a seeding rank mismatch, a broken bracket — and must be fixed. Warnings are things to review, like a team with no games, back-to-back games, or repeated matchups; some are fine for your event, some aren't.

It says a team is double-booked. How do I fix that?

That team is scheduled for two games in the same slot. Move one of them in the Rescheduler, or rebuild the division in the Schedule Hub. QA only reports the clash — you resolve it on those screens, then re-run QA.

QA flagged a rank mismatch or bracket error. Where do I go?

Those come from playoff seeding. Open Bracket Seeds and check that each slot's Seed Pool and Seed Rank point at a position that pool can actually produce. Correct the seed, then re-run QA.

The game-count summary shows teams with different numbers of games. Is that an error?

Not necessarily — the balance summaries are informational, for fairness. Some formats legitimately give teams different counts. Use them to judge whether the spread is acceptable for your event, not as a blocker.

I fixed the issues. Do I need to run QA again?

Yes. QA reflects the schedule as it was at the last check. After any move or rebuild, re-run it to confirm the fix worked and nothing new was introduced.

Everything says "All checks passed." Am I done?

The structural checks are clean — no double-bookings, no broken brackets, no unscheduled active teams. That's the green light to publish. It's still worth a final human glance at the Master Schedule, but QA has cleared the errors that are hard to catch by eye.

Tip: make QA the last step before every publish. It's the cheapest place to catch a double-booked team or a mis-seeded bracket — far cheaper than a coach catching it on game day.
