Email Log

This log is proof of send, not proof of delivery.

A row here means the system dispatched the message to those addresses. Whether an individual mailbox accepted it (vs. spam-filtered or bounced) is a separate deliverability question.

Did my message actually send?

If it appears in this log with a Sent time, yes — the system dispatched it. The log is newest-first, so a message you just sent shows at the top.

Who received a particular email?

Open the row. The detail view lists every recipient the message was addressed to, so you can confirm the right people were included.

Someone says they never got it — what do I check?

First confirm they're in the recipient list for that message (open the row). If they are, the email left the system, and a missing message is usually on the recipient's side — spam/junk folder, a full or mistyped mailbox, or their provider filtering it. The log can't force a redelivery.

Can I send or resend a message from here?

No — the Email Log is read-only. To send something new, use Bulletins for on-page announcements or the batch-email tools for direct mail.

How far back does the log go?

It holds the emails sent for this event. Scroll or sort by the Sent time to reach older messages; the most recent are always at the top.

Tip: before re-sending an announcement "because no one replied," check the log first — it's common to find the message already went out to everyone, and a duplicate blast just annoys recipients.
