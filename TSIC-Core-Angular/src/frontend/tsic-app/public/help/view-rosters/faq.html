Team Roster — questions

Everyone on the team can see this roster.

The cards — names, phone numbers, emails, and parent contacts — are visible to every player, parent, and coach on the team. Treat it as a shared team directory.

Who can see my phone number and email?

Everyone on your team's roster — players, parents, and coaches. The roster is a shared directory so teammates can reach one another. If something shouldn't be shown, update it on your account or ask the event organizer.

What are "Contact 1" and "Contact 2"?

They're the parents or guardians on a player's account — the family contacts we have on file. Use the envelope or phone button beside each to reach them directly. Cards with no family on file simply leave this section out.

How do I email the whole team, or just a few people?

For everyone, use Email All. For a few, tick the checkbox on the cards you want and choose Email Selected. Each person receives your message individually, so replies come back only to you.

A teammate's email or phone is blank or wrong.

The roster shows what's on each person's registration — blank means it isn't on file. Update your own details on your account; for someone else's, the player or parent updates their own, or the event organizer can help.

Why don't some cards show a uniform number or position?

Those appear only once they've been assigned. A missing number or position simply hasn't been set on that registration yet.

Can players see the roster, or only coaches?

Both — it's a directory for everyone on the team. Coaches and staff are marked with a badge; everyone else is a player.

The page says the roster isn't available.

The event organizer decides when team rosters are visible, and the setting can differ for players and coaches. If it's off, check back later or ask the organizer to turn it on.

I'm not on a team yet — where's my roster?

Your roster appears once you've been placed on a team; until then there's nothing to show. Placement is handled by your club or the event organizer.

Tip: tap a person's email or phone — or a parent's — to reach them in one step from your device.
