Tournament Parking Analysis

This tool estimates how many vehicles and teams will be on-site at each field complex over your tournament days, so you can plan parking and staffing before crowds show up. It reads your built schedule and projects the load hour by hour.

It's an estimate, driven by the schedule.

The projection comes from your actual games plus a few assumptions you control — so it only means something once the schedule is built.

The three assumptions you set

  • Arrival Buffer — how many minutes before their game teams show up.
  • Departure Buffer — how long they linger after it ends.
  • Cars / Team — how many vehicles a team brings, on average.

Together these decide how long each team "occupies" a complex around its games, and how that converts to cars. Tune them to match your event and the numbers update.

What it shows

  • Peak Cars On-Site and Peak Teams On-Site — the worst-case moment you need to plan for.
  • A chart and grid of load over the day, per complex — switch tabs between All Complexes and each individual complex/day.

How to use it

Set realistic buffers and a cars-per-team figure for your crowd, then read the peak at each complex. If the peak outstrips a lot's capacity, you can stagger start times in the Rescheduler, add overflow parking, or staff the busy window. It's a planning lens, not a reservation system.

Common mistakes

  • Running it before the schedule exists. With no games, there's nothing to project — build the schedule first.
  • Unrealistic assumptions. A too-low cars-per-team or too-short buffer understates the peak; set them to your actual crowd.
  • Treating the estimate as exact. It's a well-reasoned projection to plan against, not a guaranteed count.
