Tournament Parking Analysis

The numbers come from your schedule plus your assumptions.

Change the buffers or cars-per-team and the projected peak changes with them.

What do the Arrival and Departure buffers do?

They set how long a team is counted as on-site around each game — Arrival Buffer minutes before it starts and Departure Buffer minutes after it ends. Longer buffers mean teams overlap more, which raises the projected peak.

What's "Cars / Team"?

The average number of vehicles a team brings. The tool multiplies on-site teams by this number to estimate cars, so set it to match your crowd — a youth event with parents carpooling looks very different from one where every player drives.

Where do I see the busiest moment?

Peak Cars On-Site and Peak Teams On-Site are the headline numbers — the worst-case load you need capacity for. The chart and grid show how load builds and clears across the day, per complex.

The peak is higher than my lot holds. What can I do?

A few levers: stagger start times in the Rescheduler to flatten the peak, arrange overflow parking, or staff the busy window. This screen tells you where and when the crunch is so you can plan for it in advance.

It's showing nothing. Why?

The analysis is built from scheduled games — if the schedule isn't built yet, there's nothing to project. Generate the schedule first, then come back.

Tip: run this after scheduling but before you finalize start times. If a complex peaks over capacity, nudging a wave of games earlier or later in the Rescheduler is the cheapest fix — and this screen shows whether it worked.
