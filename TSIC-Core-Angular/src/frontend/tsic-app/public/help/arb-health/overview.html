ARB Health

ARB Health watches over your event's Automatic Recurrent Billing — the families paying by installment plan. It surfaces the two things that break those payment plans before they cost you money: cards that are about to expire, and payments that have already been declined. From here you can email the affected families a ready-made message telling them exactly how to fix it.

This is your safety net for recurring revenue.

Every registrant on this page is one failed charge away from a gap in payment. Working the list keeps installment plans on track instead of quietly lapsing.

The two tabs

  • Expiring Cards — families whose card on file expires this month. Their next automatic payment will fail unless they update the card first. Catch them before the charge date.
  • Behind in Payment — families whose automatic payment was already declined and now owe a balance. They need to update their card and clear what's due.

Emailing flagged families

  1. On either tab, select the registrants you want to contact (or select all).
  2. Open the email dialog and apply a template — the pre-written copy already includes the exact login-and-fix steps for that situation.
  3. Personalization tokens (player name, amount owed, login link, username) fill in per family — insert more if you edit the body.
  4. Leave notify directors on to keep staff copied, then send. A result summary shows how many sent and any that failed.

The Expiring Cards tab also offers a one-click send that warns everyone on the list with the standard expiration notice.

Common mistakes

  • Waiting until payments fail. Work the Expiring Cards tab early in the month — the whole point is to fix the card before the charge bounces.
  • Editing the fix-it steps out of the template. The numbered login steps are how families actually resolve it; keep them intact.
  • Sending with nothing selected. Emails go only to the registrants you've checked — confirm your selection before sending.
