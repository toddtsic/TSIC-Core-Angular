ARB Health

Timing is everything on the Expiring Cards tab.

A card flagged as expiring hasn't failed yet — you have a window to get it updated before the next automatic charge. Act early in the month, not after the decline.

What does ARB stand for?

Automatic Recurrent Billing — the installment payment plans families sign up for. This page monitors the health of those plans and flags the ones at risk of failing.

What's the difference between the two tabs?

Expiring Cards is preventive — the card expires this month and the next charge will fail if it isn't updated. Behind in Payment is corrective — a charge already declined and the family now owes a balance. One is "fix it before," the other "fix it after."

What do the email templates do?

They're pre-written messages matched to each situation, with the exact steps a family follows to log in and update their card (or pay the balance due). Apply one, and the tokens fill in each family's name, amount owed, and login details automatically.

What are the tokens like !PLAYER and !OWEDNOW?

Personalization placeholders. When the email is sent, each token is replaced with that recipient's real value — their player's name, the amount they owe, their login link and username. Insert them from the dialog if you customize the wording, and they'll fill in per family.

Should I leave "notify directors" on?

Usually yes — it copies directors so staff have a record of who was contacted about billing issues. Turn it off only if you deliberately want a silent send.

The one-click "send warnings" — who gets it?

Everyone currently listed on the Expiring Cards tab, using the standard expiration notice. It's the fast path for the routine monthly warning; use the select-and-compose flow when you want to pick recipients or change the message.

Tip: make ARB Health a monthly habit. A quick pass on the Expiring Cards tab near the start of each month prevents most of the declines you'd otherwise be chasing on the Behind-in-Payment tab later.
