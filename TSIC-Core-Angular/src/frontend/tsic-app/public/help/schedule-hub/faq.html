Schedule Hub

If the Build button won't run, the event isn't fully configured yet.

Dates, fields, timeslots, and pairings all have to be in place first — the setup check tells you which one is missing.

Where do I start on a brand-new event?

In Configure — it pulses "Start here" when the event has no games. Set game dates, fields, and timeslots, and make sure pairings exist for your team counts. Only then does Schedule have everything it needs to build.

What's the difference between the Schedule and Master modes?

Schedule is your working surface — you build and place games there, scoped to the event, an age group, or a single division. Master is the read view of the entire event on one grid: what players, coaches, and referees ultimately see.

I clicked Build but it only rebuilt one division. Why?

Build acts on the scope you have selected in the left navigator. If you're drilled into a division, the button reads Build/Re-Build for that division only. Click Event at the top of the navigator to build everything at once. The scope name is always printed right on the button.

My round-robin games built, but the championship games are missing.

That's expected — round-robin (pool) games generate automatically, but championship and bracket games are placed by hand so you control which slot each one lands in. The hub moves you to the affected division and shows a banner. Use Add Row to open a timeslot at the right time, then drop each game onto it.

Can I place games with the keyboard instead of dragging?

Yes. Switch to keyboard placement and clicking a game opens Rapid Placement, where you type to pick a field and a time. Prefer the mouse? Leave it on mouse mode and click the game, then click the open slot. Your preference is saved for next time.

How do I clear a schedule and start over?

Delete acts on the selected scope — a single division, an age group, or (at event scope) the whole build. Deleting frees those games' timeslots so you can rebuild. Because it's scope-aware, double-check the scope label before you confirm.

It's telling me I need pools or bracket seeds. Where are those?

Both are tools inside the hub. Pools assigns teams within an age group to pools; Bracket Seeds sets the playoff seeding that drives who meets whom. Configure them before building the games that depend on them.

Why does it say I need a desktop?

The scheduling grid relies on drag-and-drop and precise placement that don't work well on a phone or small tablet. Open the Schedule Hub on a laptop or desktop for the full experience.

Tip: always run QA before you publish. It catches the conflicts that are hard to spot by eye — a team booked twice in one slot, a field double-used — while they're still cheap to fix.
