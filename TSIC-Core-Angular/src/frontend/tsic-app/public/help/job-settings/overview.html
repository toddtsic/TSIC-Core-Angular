Job Settings

The control panel for a single event (a “job”) — how it looks, what it charges, who can register, and how it behaves in the mobile app. Settings are grouped into tabs across the top; open a tab, make your changes, and save.

How saving works

Each tab saves on its own — there is no single “save everything” button.

  • Change a field and a floating Save button appears at the bottom-right. It saves only the tab you're on.
  • A dot on a tab marks unsaved changes there, so you can see at a glance what still needs saving.
  • Switch tabs with unsaved changes and you'll be asked to discard them first — save before you move on if you want to keep them.
  • Leaving or refreshing the page with unsaved changes warns you too.

The tabs

General

Event name, display name, season, year, and the registration window. Job ID and Job Path are fixed.

Branding

Banner background and overlay images, overlay headline text, and the header logo.

Payment

Allowed payment methods, processing & eCheck fees, per-unit charges, donations, and check / mail-in details.

Comms

The From display name and address, plus the CC / BCC lists used on registration email.

Players

Player registration form, USA Lacrosse date, and the confirmation, waiver & code-of-conduct text.

Teams

Club-rep permissions (add / edit / delete), roster visibility, and team options.

Adult

Coach form and USA Lacrosse requirement, and the confirmation text for coaches, referees, and recruiters.

Scheduling

Event dates, game-clock parameters, time-zone offset, and public schedule / roster visibility.

Mobile/Store

Visibility in the TSIC-Events app, mobile features (RSVP, chat, scoring), and the store.

Dropdowns

The option lists that appear on player and team registration forms.

Registration & visibility switches live in Quick Links

The on/off switches show here, but you change them elsewhere.

“Allow Player / Team / Staff Registration” and the public schedule / roster switches appear on their tabs read-only, with a note pointing to Quick Links — that's where they're actually turned on and off, so both places always agree.

SuperUser-only settings

A padlock marks a field only a SuperUser can change — processing fees, sport / customer / billing type, recurring billing, and the store, among others. Directors can see these but not edit them.
