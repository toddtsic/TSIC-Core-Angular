US Lax Rankings

This tool pulls national USA Lacrosse rankings and attaches them to your registered teams, so the rankings show up alongside your event's teams. It does the heavy lifting by auto-matching national ranking entries to your teams by name; you review, fix the ones it wasn't sure about, and save.

The workflow

  1. Pick two age groups. Choose the national ranking source (the "scraped" list) and the matching registered age group in your event. It auto-aligns as soon as both are chosen.
  2. Review the matches. Each of your teams shows the ranking it was paired with and a confidence — high, medium, or low — based on how closely the names matched.
  3. Fix the uncertain ones. For a team that didn't match (or matched wrong), click it, then pick the correct ranking from the sidebar. You can also undo a match to send it back.
  4. Save. Choose a threshold — high-confidence only, high + medium, or everything — and the rankings are written onto those teams. Manual matches you made are always saved too.
Confidence tells you how much to trust an auto-match.

High = the names lined up cleanly. Medium = probably right, worth a glance. Low / unmatched = check it yourself before saving. Start by saving high-confidence, then work through the rest by hand.

Other actions

  • Filter the table to unmatched, high, or medium to focus your review.
  • Export CSV to get the alignment as a spreadsheet.
  • Clear rankings removes saved rankings from the selected age group's teams — a clean slate if an import went wrong.

Common mistakes

  • Saving everything blindly. "All" includes low-confidence guesses. Review low/unmatched first, or you'll pin the wrong ranking on a team.
  • Mismatched age groups. Aligning a U15 national list against your U13 teams produces nonsense matches — pair like with like.
  • Forgetting to save. Reviewing and matching on screen isn't persisted until you Save; leave without saving and the work is lost.
