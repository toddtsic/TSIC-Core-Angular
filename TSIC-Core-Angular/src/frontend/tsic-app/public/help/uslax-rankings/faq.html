Auto-matching is a suggestion, not a guarantee.

It pairs by team name, and names are messy. Always review low-confidence and unmatched rows before you save, or you'll attach the wrong ranking to a team.

What do high, medium, and low confidence mean?

They reflect how closely a national ranking entry's name matched one of your teams. High lined up cleanly, medium is a likely match worth a glance, and low (or unmatched) needs you to confirm or fix it by hand.

How do I match a team the tool missed?

Click the team row to put it in match mode, then click the correct ranking in the sidebar. That pairs them as a manual match. If a team was matched to the wrong ranking, do the same to reassign it — the old ranking goes back to the sidebar.

What's the difference between the save thresholds?

High saves only high-confidence auto-matches (plus your manual ones). Medium adds the medium-confidence matches. All saves everything, including low-confidence guesses. Manual matches you made are always included regardless of threshold.

I saved the wrong rankings — how do I start over?

Use Clear rankings for the selected registered age group. It removes the saved ranking data from those teams so you can re-align and save fresh.

Nothing aligned — did it fail?

Usually it means the two age groups don't correspond, or the national source has no entries for that group. Confirm you picked the right national list and the right registered age group, then re-align.

Tip: save high-confidence first, then filter the table to unmatched and work that short list by hand. It's far faster than scanning every row, and you won't miss a team.
