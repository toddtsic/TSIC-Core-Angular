LADT editor — FAQ

What does "LADT" stand for?

Leagues, Age Groups, Divisions, Teams — the four levels of the event tree, in the order they nest. A league contains age groups, an age group contains divisions, and a division contains teams.

How do I add an age group, division, or team?

Hover the parent branch and click its button — a blank row appears right where the new item will live. Type a name and press Enter to create it, or Esc to back out. You add age groups from a league, divisions from an age group, and teams from a division.

Why can't I delete this age group or division?

An age group or division can be removed only when it has no teams beneath it — the button is hidden while any remain, so you don't strand teams. Move or remove the teams first. The Unassigned division is permanent and can never be deleted.

What happens when I remove a team?

It depends on whether the team has any history. If it has no players, payments, or schedule records, it's deleted permanently. If it does, it isn't erased — it's moved to the Dropped Teams bucket and deactivated, so its registrations and payment trail stay intact.

Why is a fee shown faded, or labeled "inherited"?

Fees cascade down the tree — Job → League → Age Group → Team — and the most specific level that actually sets a value wins. A faded pill means this row didn't set that fee itself; it's inherited from a level above. Hover the to see whether it was set here or inherited, and from which level.

What do "Single", "Deposit", and "PIF" mean in the Payment Phase column?
Single Deposit PIF

Single — one charge, because the fee has only a deposit or only a balance. Deposit — a deposit now with the balance due later. PIF (paid in full) — the whole amount is required up front. The phase only matters when a fee carries both a deposit and a balance to split; otherwise it always reads Single.

What does "Theme Division Names" do?

It's a bulk way to name divisions the same across every age group. Open it from the menu, enter a list of names (classic Pool A, Pool B or any theme), and each age group's divisions are renamed to match — creating new divisions where an age group has fewer than your list. It shows a full preview first, and a division that already has teams is locked so it won't be renamed away.

What are the "Dropped Teams" and "WAITLIST" buckets?

They're holding areas, not real age groups — so they sit at the bottom of the tree and look different. Dropped Teams collects teams removed from active play (with their history preserved), and WAITLIST holds teams awaiting a spot. You won't add or edit divisions inside them the way you would a normal age group.

Is there a fast way to move between teams while editing?

Yes. With the detail panel open, use the ▲ / ▼ arrow keys or the dropdown in the panel header to step through siblings without closing it. If you have unsaved edits, it asks before moving on so nothing is lost.

Why does the Club column sometimes not appear?

The Club column shows only when the event actually has teams from more than one club. For a single-club event it's hidden to keep the grid clean.

How do I recolor an age group?

Click the color dot to the left of the age group's name in the tree and pick a swatch (or "none"). The color applies immediately and flows down to that age group's divisions, so related items read together in the tree and in scheduling.

Tip: setting a fee once at the league or age group level lets every team below inherit it — you only drop to team-level fees for the exceptions.
