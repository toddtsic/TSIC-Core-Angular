The LADT editor

LADT is the structural backbone of an event — its Leagues, Age Groups, Divisions, and Teams. This screen is where you build and maintain that hierarchy: add and rename branches, set fees, recolor age groups, and edit a single team without losing your place.

The four levels

Every event is a tree four levels deep. Each level is the parent of the one below it:

League Age Group Division Team

How it works

  1. Pick any item in the tree on the left.
  2. The grid on the right lists everything at that level — the item's siblings — with their counts and fees.
  3. Click a row (or a tree item) to open its detail panel, where you edit its fields.

The header shows event-wide totals — teams and players — plus how many active teams are waitlisted, scheduled, or neither. Use Expand / Collapse to open or close the whole tree, and Refresh to reload it.

Building the structure

Hover a branch and click the to add a child — an age group under a league, a division under an age group, a team under a division. A new row appears inline; type a name and press Enter to keep it or Esc to cancel.

From the grid you can also Clone a team or a whole age group — a fast way to stand up a second bracket or a returning division without rebuilding it by hand.

Fees at a glance

For leagues, age groups, and teams the grid shows the resolved Deposit / Balance, Early Bird Discount, Late Fee, and Payment Phase. Fees cascade down the tree — Job → League → Age Group → Team — and the most specific level that sets a value wins. A pill shown faded is inherited from a level above; hover its to see exactly where it came from.

Single Deposit PIF

Payment Phase tells you how a registrant pays: Single — one charge (the fee has only a deposit or only a balance); Deposit — pay a deposit now, the balance later; PIF — the full amount is required up front. The phase only splits when a fee carries both a deposit and a balance.

Colors & division themes

Click the color dot beside an age group to give it a color — its divisions pick up the same tint, so the tree (and later, schedules) reads at a glance. The gear menu's Theme Division Names names divisions across every age group at once: type a set of names (Pool A, Pool B… or a fun theme) and each age group's divisions are renamed to match, with new ones created where an age group is short. It previews every change before you apply it.

Removing things

  • Leagues can't be deleted here.
  • Age groups and divisions can be deleted only when no teams sit beneath them; the is hidden otherwise. The Unassigned division is permanent.
  • Removing a team is safe: if it has no players, payments, or schedule history it's deleted outright; otherwise it's moved to Dropped Teams and deactivated so its records survive.

Good to know

  • Dropped Teams and WAITLIST buckets always sort to the bottom of the tree and are styled apart — they're holding areas, not editable age groups.
  • With the detail panel open, step through siblings with the ▲ / ▼ keys or the dropdown in its header — no need to close and reopen. Unsaved edits prompt before you leave.
  • The Club column appears only when the event has teams from more than one club.
