Searching registrations — frequently asked

Real answers for finding, contacting, and managing the individual people registered in your event.

Filters don't apply until you press Search.
Set your criteria, then press — closing the drawer with unapplied changes will prompt you to run it.

I changed my filters but the results didn't update. Why?

Because filters take effect only when you press Search — see the note above. Your active filters then appear as removable chips above the results, so you can always see, and clear, exactly what's applied.

I picked a filter and it changed the role for me — is that a bug?

No. A few filters narrow the role automatically because they only make sense for one kind of registrant — an insurance filter, for example, implies you're looking at players. Some filters also appear only when the event uses that feature (RegSaver insurance, or ARB recurring billing).

What do the red amount and the calendar icon in the Owed column mean?

$250 owed Auto-pay scheduled

A red amount means a balance is due; a small calendar icon means an automatic payment is already scheduled for an upcoming date. The footer totals Paid and Owed across the whole result set.

Why are some rows struck through?

Those registrations are inactive. The strikethrough styling makes them easy to pick out among the active ones.

How do I email just some people, not everyone?

Check the rows you want and use Email Selected to open the batch composer for just those people. Email All composes to the entire result set.

Where do I refund a card payment or delete a registration?

Click a person's name to open their detail panel. From there you can update contact info and demographics, process a credit-card refund, or delete the registration.

Why don't I see the Invite button?

It appears only when your search is scoped to a single invitable role — Player or Club Rep — since an invitation is tied to that role.

What does "Look up CCs expiring this month" do?

For events with recurring billing, it runs a live query against the card processor and returns everyone with a card about to expire — deliberately ignoring your current filters — so you can collect balances before an automatic payment fails.

Tip: the filter chips above the results are your safety check — if a search looks wrong, glance at the chips before assuming the data is off. Usually a stray filter is still applied.
