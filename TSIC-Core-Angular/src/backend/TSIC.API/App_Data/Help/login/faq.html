Signing in — frequently asked

The things people get stuck on at the sign-in screen, and how to get past them.

The message "Invalid username or password" is deliberately vague.
For security it won't tell you which of the two was wrong — so check both, starting with the username.

I'm certain my password is right, but it still says invalid. Why?

The error covers both fields, so a wrong username shows the same message as a wrong password. Check the username spelling, watch for Caps Lock and spaces, and — if you're still stuck — reset the password with "Forgot your password?" to rule it out.

Do I sign in with my email, or a username?

The top field is your username. For some accounts that username is an email address; for others it's a name you chose. Enter whatever you set up when the account was created — not necessarily your email.

The red error message disappeared while I was reading it.

That's by design — the error clears the moment you edit either field, so you can try again cleanly. It vanishing does not mean the sign-in went through; you'll know it worked when the page moves on.

I forgot my password.

Click "Forgot your password?" under the form and enter the email on file for your account. You'll get a reset link at that address.

Is there a minimum password length?

Yes — passwords are at least six characters. A very old, shorter password won't be accepted, which is another good reason to reset it.

After I sign in, I get a "pick a registration" screen. Is that right?

Yes. Because one login can hold several registrations — different children, teams, or roles — the "Open a Registration" screen lets you choose which one to work in. It's a normal step, not an error.

I have accounts for more than one child or team. Do I need separate logins?

No. Sign in once; everything on your account appears together on the next screen, where you pick the registration you want.

Tip: still locked out after resetting the password? The event's contact — listed on the event's landing page — can confirm the right username for your account.
